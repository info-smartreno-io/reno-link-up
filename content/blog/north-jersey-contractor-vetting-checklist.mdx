Run this **North Jersey**‑ready checklist before you sign:

## Essential Verification Steps

### 1. License & Insurance Verification

<Callout type="error" title="Never Skip This Step">
Verify your contractor has an active **NJ Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) license** and current liability insurance. Request certificates and verify directly with the issuer.
</Callout>

- Check HIC license number at nj.gov
- Verify general liability insurance ($1M+ recommended)
- Confirm workers' compensation coverage
- Ask for certificate of insurance

### 2. Local References

Get at least 3 references from projects in your area:
- Comparable local references (e.g., **Teaneck, Wayne, Morristown, Maplewood, Jersey City**)
- Similar scope and budget range
- Completed within last 12 months
- Contact references directly (not just read online reviews)

### 3. Contract Essentials

<Callout type="warning" title="Red Flags">
Walk away if the contractor asks for more than 10% deposit, refuses to provide references, or pressures you to sign immediately.
</Callout>

Your contract must include:
- Clear scope & exclusions
- Detailed payment schedule (milestone-based)
- Change order process and communication plan
- Written warranty with specific term (minimum 1 year)
- Start and completion dates
- Permit responsibility

### 4. Financial Red Flags

Watch out for:
- Requests for large upfront payments (>10%)
- Cash-only deals
- Pressure to decide immediately
- No written contract offered
- Vague scope descriptions

## Questions to Ask

1. How long have you been in business?
2. Do you have experience with [specific project type] in [your town]?
3. Who will be the on-site supervisor?
4. What is your typical project timeline for this scope?
5. How do you handle change orders?
6. What warranty do you provide?
