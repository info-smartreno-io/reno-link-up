## HELOC Overview

<Callout type="success" title="Best for Large Projects">
**HELOC:** lower rates, flexible draws; best for larger, staged scopes in **Bergen and Morris County** homes with significant equity.
</Callout>

**HELOC advantages:**
- Lower interest rates (typically prime + 0–2%)
- Only pay interest on what you draw
- Flexible access during draw period
- Can reuse as you pay down

**HELOC considerations:**
- Requires home appraisal
- Variable interest rates
- Approval takes 3–6 weeks
- Closing costs ($500–$2,000)

## Home Improvement Loan

<Callout type="info" title="Best for Mid-Range Projects">
**Home improvement loan:** fast approval; good for mid-range baths/kitchens in **Passaic and Essex County**. Fixed rates provide predictable payments.
</Callout>

**Loan advantages:**
- Fast approval (1–2 weeks)
- Fixed interest rate and payment
- No home appraisal needed
- Lower closing costs

**Loan considerations:**
- Higher rates than HELOC
- Fixed loan amount (can't draw more)
- Shorter repayment terms
- Personal credit score is key factor

## Alternative Options

**Promotional credit cards:** small items only; avoid interest traps.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid These Pitfalls">
Promotional credit card offers can work for small purchases under $5,000, but carrying balances past the promo period results in expensive interest charges.
</Callout>

## Which Option is Right?

**Choose HELOC if:**
- Project cost over $50,000
- Phased renovation (kitchen, then bath)
- Strong home equity (20%+)
- Comfortable with variable rates

**Choose home improvement loan if:**
- Project cost $15,000–$50,000
- Single project scope
- Want fixed payments
- Need faster approval
