## What Bergen County Towns Require

<Callout type="info" title="Town-Specific Requirements">
**Paramus, Ridgewood, and Fort Lee** have similar but not identical requirements. Always verify with your specific building department before starting work.
</Callout>

**Expect:**  
- **Electrical** (GFCI, circuits, lighting plan)
- **Plumbing** (sink/disposal, DW, supply/drain)
- **Mechanical** (range vent, makeup air where required)
- **Inspections**: rough + final; condo buildings add HOA requirements

## Electrical Requirements

<Callout type="warning" title="GFCI Protection Required">
All countertop receptacles must have GFCI protection. This is strictly enforced in **Bergen County** inspections.
</Callout>

**Common electrical needs:**
- Dedicated 20-amp circuits for appliances
- GFCI protection at all countertop outlets
- Proper lighting on dedicated circuits
- Updated panel if needed for load
- Arc-fault protection for new circuits

## Plumbing Requirements

**Typical plumbing permits cover:**
- Sink and disposal connections
- Dishwasher supply and drain
- Ice maker line if applicable
- Gas line for range (if applicable)
- Proper venting for all fixtures

## Mechanical & Ventilation

<Callout type="success" title="Ventilation Matters">
Proper range hood ventilation improves air quality and prevents moisture damage. Many Bergen County towns have specific CFM requirements based on cooktop BTU output.
</Callout>

**Range hood requirements:**
- Minimum CFM based on range size
- Ducted to exterior (recirculating often not allowed)
- Makeup air if over 400 CFM (some towns)
- Proper clearances to combustibles

## Inspection Process

### Rough Inspection

Scheduled after framing/blocking but before walls close:
- Electrical boxes and wiring visible
- Plumbing rough-in accessible
- Proper blocking for cabinets/fixtures
- Ventilation ductwork installed

### Final Inspection

After all work complete:
- All fixtures properly installed
- GFCI outlets tested and working
- Proper clearances maintained
- Ventilation operational
- Code-compliant finishes

## Fort Lee Condo Considerations

<Callout type="warning" title="Condo Buildings Add Requirements">
Fort Lee condos require both municipal permits AND HOA approval. Factor in additional time for architectural review and building-specific rules.
</Callout>

**Additional condo requirements:**
- HOA architectural approval (2–4 weeks)
- Building insurance certificate
- Work hour restrictions
- Elevator reservations
- Hallway protection plan
