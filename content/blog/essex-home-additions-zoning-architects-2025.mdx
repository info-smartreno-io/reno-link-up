## Zoning Fundamentals

<Callout type="warning" title="Know Your Zoning First">
**Zoning basics:** setbacks, lot coverage, FAR (Floor Area Ratio). Many Essex towns (Montclair, Maplewood, Livingston) have **historic or neighborhood design** rules — align early to avoid costly redesigns.
</Callout>

**Key zoning considerations:**
- **Setback requirements** (front, side, rear)
- **Lot coverage limits** (percentage of lot that can be built on)
- **FAR maximums** (total building area vs. lot size)
- **Height restrictions**
- **Historic district requirements** (Montclair, South Orange)

## Required Plan Set

<Callout type="info" title="Professional Plans Required">
**Plan set** typically includes: site plan, floor plans, elevations/sections, structural notes, MEP scope outline. Most towns require architect or engineer stamps for additions.
</Callout>

**Typical drawings include:**
- Survey and site plan
- Existing and proposed floor plans
- Exterior elevations (all sides)
- Building sections
- Structural details
- Mechanical/electrical/plumbing plans

## Timeline Expectations

**Timeline:** design 3–6 weeks, permitting 3–8 weeks, build 8–20+ weeks depending on scale.

<Callout type="success" title="Plan for Success">
Budget extra time for zoning variance hearings if needed. Essex County towns can take 6–12 weeks for variance approvals, but proper planning often avoids this step.
</Callout>

## Common Addition Types

- **Bump-out (under 200 sq ft):** Often simplified approval
- **Second story addition:** Requires full structural review
- **Primary suite addition:** Popular in Livingston, Millburn
- **Family room extension:** Common in Maplewood, West Orange
